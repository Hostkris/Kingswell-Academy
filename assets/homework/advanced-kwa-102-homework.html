Big Idea

Writing & Clear Communication is about learning how to move from loose thoughts to clean paragraphs. The student should finish the class able to explain the idea, show an example, correct mistakes, and save proof.

Written Response

Practice Drills

  • Define sentence in your own words and connect it to complete sentences.
  • Choose one example of paragraph structure from writing a short school explanation or email and explain the evidence.
  • Find one common mistake students make with topic sentence and correct it.
  • Create portfolio proof for Writing & Clear Communication.

Worked Examples to Review

  • Guided example: Complete sentences: Name the target skill: complete sentences.; Find where it appears in writing a short school explanation or email.; Use one vocabulary term such as sentence or paragraph to explain it.; Write one correction: what was unclear, wrong, missing, or unsupported?; Save the proof as a paragraph draft + edited final copy.
  • Transfer example: Supporting detail: Choose a fresh example involving supporting detail.; Complete the task once with guidance.; Mark one mistake or weak sentence/problem/decision.; Fix it and explain why the fix is better.; Teach the idea back in two or three plain sentences.

Project Proof

Write and revise a one-page explanation essay

  • Pick one real situation connected to writing a short school explanation or email.
  • Use at least four course terms: sentence, paragraph, thesis, outline.
  • Create a first attempt, then revise it after feedback.
  • Submit the final paragraph draft + edited final copy and a short reflection.

Deliverable: Final deliverable: paragraph draft + edited final copy plus a one-paragraph reflection explaining what improved.

Self-Check Rubric

  • Concept understanding: Mastery - Explains complete sentences and paragraph structure clearly in plain language.; Still developing - Names the topic but cannot explain how it works.
  • Application: Mastery - Uses the skill correctly in writing a short school explanation or email.; Still developing - Only repeats the classroom example or guesses.
  • Correction: Mastery - Finds one error, fixes it, and explains why the new answer is stronger.; Still developing - Changes the answer without explaining the reason.
  • Portfolio proof: Mastery - Submits a complete paragraph draft + edited final copy with reflection.; Still developing - Submits incomplete proof or a screenshot with no thinking shown.
